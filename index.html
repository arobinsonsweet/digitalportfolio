About

I am a PhD candidate in Information Studies at UCLA and have a background in archival studies, community organizing, and visual art. My research lies at the intersection of critical archival studies and critical prison studies, and examines how communities impacted by state violence in the United States use archives and records for accountability beyond the confines of the criminal legal system.

As an abolitionist, I seek to uplift liberatory memory work and build archival practice for a future without prisons or police. At UCLA, I work with the Community Archives Lab and teach in the Labor Studies program. Previously, I  was an archivist at The New School Archives. Outside of the academy and the archive, I am involved in abolitionist movement work and create art about public history, memory, and liberation. Currently, I organize with the Stop LAPD Spying Coalition and the Prison Library Support Network.
